The Stormcaller of the Riven Coast — an original test fixture (synthetic English lore)

Alaric Stormcaller is a weather-mage of the Riven Coast, a salt-bitten land of
black cliffs and perpetual gales where the sea-wardens keep the old pacts. He is
tall and gaunt, his grey cloak stitched with copper threads that hum faintly before
a storm, and his eyes are the pale blue of sheet lightning. A jagged scar runs from
his left brow to his jaw, the mark of the night the Tempest Court first answered his
call. He wears no armour, only a circle of weather-glass beads at his throat that
darken when danger nears.

By temperament Alaric is patient and watchful, slow to anger but pitiless once
roused, for he believes that mercy spent on a tyrant is theft from the tyrant's
victims. He distrusts kings and courts alike, and keeps his counsel close; yet to
the fisher-folk of the Riven Coast he is unfailingly gentle, having been a drowned
sailor's orphan himself before the storms claimed him as their own.

His powers are bound to wind and water: he can summon squalls to scatter a fleet,
still a hurricane to a flat grey calm, and read the coming weather three days hence
in the behaviour of the gulls. The copper threads of his cloak let him hurl a single
bolt of lightning, but the cost is dear — each casting leaves him cold and shaking
for an hour after. He cannot make rain where there is no moisture, and iron worked
by a smith who has never seen the sea will turn his bolts aside.

Alaric answers to no lord but holds an uneasy alliance with Maren of the Tide-Keep,
the harbour-mistress who shelters him, and a long feud with the Ashen Conclave, the
fire-priests of the inland cities who would see all weather-magic burned out of the
world. In his youth he served as a storm-pilot guiding merchant hulls through the
Drowned Teeth; the wreck that scarred him also taught him the pacts of the Tempest
Court, and he has wandered the coast as its reluctant warden ever since.
