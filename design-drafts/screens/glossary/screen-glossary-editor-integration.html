Chapter 3: The Clerk's Bargain

Aldric stepped through the ancient archway into the Hall of Records, his worn boots echoing on marble floors that had witnessed a thousand years of bureaucratic diligence. The air smelled of cedar and old ink — the unmistakable perfume of the Clerk's Guild.

"You're late," said Magistrate Voss, not looking up from a stack of requisition forms. She adjusted her Seal of Office — a heavy bronze medallion that could authorize anything from a tax exemption to an execution.

Aldric set the Moonblade on the desk. Its pale light flickered against the Ward Sigils carved into every surface of the room. "I need this registered," he said. "As a Class-VII Arcane Artifact."

Voss finally looked up, one eyebrow rising behind wire-rimmed spectacles. "A Class-VII? In the Ancient Capital? Do you have any idea how much paperwork that involves?"
