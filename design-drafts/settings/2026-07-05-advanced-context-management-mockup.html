Context Management

Expert

Control how the assistant remembers your story and manages long conversations. You rarely need to change anything here — the defaults are tuned for most books.

What this is, in plain terms

As you write together, the assistant keeps two things in mind: your story world — the characters, places, and facts it has learned — and your recent conversation. On a very large book those can grow past what fits, so it quietly summarizes the oldest messages to stay fast and keep your token cost down. Your original messages are never deleted; only the copy the model reads is condensed.

These settings tune how that happens. For almost everyone, Auto is right — you can close this page. Open the expert controls only if you're working on a very large novel, or you specifically want to trade cost and speed against how much the assistant holds in mind.

The 60-second explainer

Grounding is the story context looked up each turn so the assistant doesn't forget your world. Compaction is what happens when a conversation runs long: the oldest turns become a short recap so the newest turns still fit the model's window. On a small book this almost never fires; on a huge one it fires often — and the tiers below make it cheaper and safer when it does.

Before you change these

The defaults came from live measurement. On a small book, turning tiers on adds cost and latency with no quality gain; the payoff only shows on large works where the assistant actually runs out of room. When in doubt, leave the mode on Auto.

Long-work context mode

The one switch most people touch — it decides whether the memory & compression tiers turn on.

Auto. The assistant switches the tiers on by itself once a book is big enough to need them, and stays out of the way otherwise. Recommended.

Auto switches on when any of these is true
  • the book is over 500,000 words, or
  • it has more than 150 chapters, or
  • the story bible passes 300 glossary entities, or
  • a typical session is projected to fill more than 60% of the model window.
▸ Fine-tune individual tiers Expert

Override the automatic behavior tier by tier. Each maps to a Context Budget Law tier; the code is shown for anyone cross-referencing the docs, and would sit behind “Technical detail” in the real panel.

Smart grounding fetch T5
Skip the story-lore lookup on turns that plainly don't need it — small talk, planning, “what can you do?”. Saves time and tokens on chit-chat.
When to use: large story bibles where every lookup is expensive.
Technical detail

Gates build_context retrieval by detected intent. Meaningful only when grounding is costly per turn (pull-mode); with today's inline grounding it saves ~0 tokens, so it stays off unless the book is very large.

Story-bible safety net T4
Keeps a compact, always-available copy of your story bible so the assistant never loses the thread on a turn where it skipped the full lookup.
When to use: alongside Smart grounding fetch — it's the net beneath it.
Turns on with “Smart grounding fetch”
Technical detail

Projects a cached, bounded (~1,200-token) bible block only when the live grounding prefix is empty — a gated or degraded turn. Inert unless T5 gating is on.

Trim repeated lookups T6 · D13a
When the conversation is compressed, drop duplicate copies of the same lookup — the latest copy is kept, so nothing is lost, it just saves room.
When to use: long sessions that re-read the same chapters or entities often.
Technical detail

On a compaction pass, collapses exact-duplicate tool results to a reference. Reversible (the raw stays in the database) and can't orphan a tool pair. Fires only when compaction has already triggered.

Compression trigger threshold
Start summarizing older messages once the conversation reaches this share of the model's context window.
Default 75%. Lower compresses sooner (cheaper, more summarizing); higher keeps more raw history (richer, riskier near the limit).
