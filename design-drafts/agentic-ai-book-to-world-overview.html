Concept overview

From your book to a living world

LoreWeave helps authors write and refine novels with AI assistance. The same manuscript data powers translation, chat, and a structured lore library—and lays the groundwork for a long-term vision where readers can step inside the worlds you create.

1

System architecture (high level)

Think of the platform as layers: you interact through the web app; one gateway coordinates requests; specialized services handle books, billing, AI routing, and knowledge; trusted storage keeps text, files, and—when enabled—a structured map of your story world.

People

Authors and readers use a browser or device.

Web app

Editor, catalog, chat, settings—one front door for the product.

↓

API gateway (BFF)

A single entry point for outside traffic: routing, auth handoff, and service coordination.

↓

Product & AI services

Behind the gateway, many small services work together: books and chapters, sharing and catalog, provider credentials and usage, translation and notifications, chat and knowledge extraction—without exposing that complexity in everyday use.

↓

Storage

Manuscripts and metadata in databases; media in object storage; an optional knowledge graph for entities and relationships when you enable deeper lore features.

Your keys, your models. AI calls use your own provider keys (BYOK) where configured—so costs and model choice stay under your control.

2

Pipeline: book → lore → future play

Writing a chapter is only the start. In the background, the system can hand text to multi-step AI workflows that enrich a shared lore layer. Today that powers assistants and tools; a future design explores stepping into those worlds as structured play.

Write

Authors draft and save chapters in the editor; content is stored safely as the source of truth.

→

Handoff

Saves trigger reliable background processing so extraction and other jobs do not block typing.

→

Extract

AI reads chapters in stages—finding entities, relationships, and facts that match your project settings.

→

Lore layer

Glossary, wiki-style articles, and a knowledge graph connect story text to structured lore.

→

Today

Chat, translation, continuity-aware assistance—grounded in your canon when enabled.

Future

Living Worlds: text-first experiences where NPCs and narration draw from the same lore—explored in planning, not yet a shipped product surface.

What “agentic AI” means here

  • Multi-step background work — not just one reply: queued reading, model calls, and merging results into your lore store over time.
  • Respects your rules — usage limits, project toggles, and provider choices shape what runs and when.
  • Grounded in the manuscript — extractions aim to trace back to passages so “who said what” stays inspectable, not magical memory.
