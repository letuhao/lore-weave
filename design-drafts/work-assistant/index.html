Design principles (from the spec)

  • No writer vocabulary. The user never sees "chapter", "publish", "entity", "kind", or "inbox". They see Entry, Keep, People I noticed, Worth remembering (S06 no-jargon rule).
  • Capture is visible, not silent. The "today so far" rail makes what's captured tangible every turn — the honest answer to the "is it actually collecting?" failure mode. Nothing is saved until the nightly review.
  • One calm review moment. Entry + both inboxes + reflection collapse into a single end-of-day step with a bulk "Keep all" and a ≤10-decisions budget, so the memory never starves from review fatigue.
  • Week-1 value before the graph fills. Raw recall ("what did I say about the launch?") works day one; the diary and "what I know" surfaces visibly fill up over time.
  • Private by construction. The diary is kind-locked to private — a lock chip, no share button, grants and public-visibility blocked server-side.
  • Calm, personal tone. Warm palette, serif display, first-person assistant voice — a diary, not a dashboard.
  • Mobile-first. These are the phone's daily surfaces; the reused book GUI needs a real mobile pass (sidebar → drawer/bottom-nav), not just the chat.

How this feeds the spec

Mockups sharpen three things the spec left open: (a) the "today so far" rail is a concrete home for the capture-decision data path (PUX-5) — worth adding as a P1 surface, not just a status chip; (b) the end-of-day review confirms folding the three human gates into one screen (PUX-2/3) rather than routing to writer panels; (c) the reflection/coaching screen shows the P5 longitudinal leg needs a "what I know about you" knowledge view, which is a good week-1 empty-state anchor too.

Open questions surfaced by the drafts: does the activity heatmap/streak help or add pressure? Is the nightly review opt-in or a gentle nudge? Should "what I know about your work" be editable inline (forget/correct) from this view?
