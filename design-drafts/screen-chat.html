Character development — Aldric

Claude Sonnet 4.6 · 8 messages

I'm developing the Hero character (Aldric) for my novel. He's supposed to be the classic "chosen hero" archetype but with a twist — he's bureaucratic and files formal complaints. Can you help me flesh out his personality and backstory?

That's a fantastic subversion of the hero trope! Here's a framework for Aldric:

Core Personality: Aldric genuinely believes in justice and doing what's right — but he also believes deeply in process. He was raised by retired paladins who ran a legal practice. To him, slaying the Demon Lord without proper authorization is as wrong as the Demon Lord's crimes.

Backstory:

  • Trained at the Royal Academy of Heroes and the Clerk's Guild simultaneously
  • His legendary sword "Precedent" has a filing system built into the pommel
  • He carries a copy of the "Hero-Demon Lord Engagement Protocol" — a document he co-authored

Key Conflict: What happens when the bureaucratic process fails to deliver justice? When does Aldric break his own rules?

I love the idea of "Precedent" as the sword name! What would his relationship with the Demon Lord look like if they've been doing this dance for years?
