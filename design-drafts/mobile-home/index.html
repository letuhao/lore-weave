5 · Push · ask

Earn the prompt, then ask

A soft pre-prompt shown after the user has seen value — never on first launch — that says exactly what they’ll get and, critically, what they won’t (no diary text). “Turn on” then triggers the OS permission.

WhyA blind day-one OS prompt gets denied and can’t be re-asked. Framing the value first — and the content-free promise — is what earns the yes; “Not now” leaves it re-askable later.

6 · Push · delivered

Content-free on the lock screen

What actually lands, even with the app closed: a stable title and “there’s something to see” — no names, no notes, no decisions. Two examples: the assistant check-in and a finished job.

WhyThis is R3’s discipline made literal: the push carries a title + an i18n key, never diary text — safe over a shoulder or on a shared device. Tapping routes into the right screen.
