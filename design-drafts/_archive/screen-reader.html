Chapter 3

The Hero's Complaint

The throne room was silent except for the gentle hiss of magical torches. The Demon Lord sat with one leg crossed over the other, examining a scroll that detailed the latest territorial dispute with the neighboring kingdom of Aeloria.

"Your Majesty, the Hero has arrived," announced the chamberlain, his voice echoing against the obsidian walls. The words carried an undertone of anxiety that even centuries of service could not fully mask.

The Demon Lord sighed — a sound that caused the torches to flicker. "Send him in. And prepare the tea. The good one, not the cursed variety." A pause. "Actually, prepare both. Let's see what kind of mood he's in."

The doors burst open with theatrical force. The Hero strode in, armor gleaming, cape billowing despite the notable absence of wind. His sword was drawn, pointed directly at the ceiling in what was clearly a rehearsed pose.

"Demon Lord!" he bellowed. "I have come to lodge a formal complaint!" The Hero reached into his breastplate and produced a crumpled piece of parchment. "Subsection 7, paragraph 3 of the Hero-Demon Lord Engagement Protocol clearly states—"

"Sit down, Aldric." The Demon Lord gestured to a chair that materialized from shadow. "Tea?"

The Hero's dramatic stance faltered. His sword arm drooped slightly. "Is it the Aeloran blend? The one with the mint?"

"It is."

Aldric sheathed his sword with practiced fluidity and sat down, his armor clanking against the conjured chair. He placed the crumpled parchment on the table between them, smoothing it with the care of a man who believed deeply in proper documentation.

"Now then," the Demon Lord said, pouring two cups with a silver teapot that floated at exactly the right height. "What seems to be the problem this time?"

"This time?" Aldric's eyes narrowed. "You say that as if my complaints are trivial."

"I say it as someone who has received forty-seven formal complaints in the past year alone. Thirteen of which concerned the font size on your copies of the Engagement Protocol."

Aldric took a measured sip of tea. "Legibility is not trivial."
