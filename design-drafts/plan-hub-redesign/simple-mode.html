the doorWrite a new chapter creates the chapter AND opens the editor — the "just start writing" entry the pantser and the newcomer both said was missing. No need to choose an arc first.
no jargonThe word "arc" never appears in Simple mode. A newcomer starts against "chapter", which everyone already understands. Arcs are an Advanced concept, introduced only when the writer opts in.
same modelThese are real chapters (book chapter + outline_node). Unassigned to any arc, they sit in the UnplannedTray that already exists — switch to Advanced and drag them into storylines. Structure emerges FROM the prose, not before it.
keptThe authorship coding survives, quietly: your titles are Lora serif; the one AI-suggested chapter (5) is mono + an "AI idea" tag. The panel's unanimous keep, carried into Simple.
cutEvery telemetry chip (100/340, ⚠ gap, 4 ch). Status is one quiet dot + a word; word count is the one number a writer actually wants. Nothing here reads like Jira.
scaleA list scales to 10k chapters natively — it scrolls, and the whole book is legible top-to-bottom (the "see the shape" the plotter wanted, which the paginated canvas couldn't give). Keyset paging still loads windows underneath.
defaultSimple is the DEFAULT for a new writer (per-user setting, like the shipped MotifSimpleMode). Advanced — the lane canvas — is the toggle top-right, for when a plotter wants to build structure.
