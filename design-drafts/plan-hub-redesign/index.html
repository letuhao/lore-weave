AI · collapsed
root fix 1Cards are no longer 128px truncated. The arc name is a full, wrapping headline; chapter titles clamp to 2 lines at 190px; scenes are readable chips. You can finally see what your structure IS.
root fix 2The hierarchy renders by default: arc → chapter → scene, all visible. Today the canvas collapses every arc to a rollup and hides the rest — a graph canvas that shows only the top level wastes the one thing a canvas is good at. Collapse still exists (arc 3, ▸) — it's opt-in, not the default.
keptThe lanes — the PO is right that they're the correct model. An arc is a horizontal lane; its chapters sit in it left-to-right in reading order; scenes nest under chapters. Sub-arcs are inset lanes (arc 2).
droppedThe killed Spine's overlap band. Arcs can't overlap (structure_node_id is a scalar FK), so there's nothing to draw. This renders the real single-parent tree — and reads cleaner for it.
authorshipArc 3 is mono + teal — the planner proposed it and you haven't adopted it. The "AI: proposed bridge scene" chip in ch 2 is the same rule at scene level. You always see which marks are yours.
createEvery level has its inline add, where the thing will live: + chapter at the end of a lane, + scene in a chapter, + arc/+ sub-arc on the toolbar. Wired to the routes already verified (arc→chapter→scene all create live).
non-contigArc 2's ⚠ gap ch 5 chip is the real is_contiguous=false signal — the arc owns ch 3 and 6 but not 5. Shown as a chip, not a broken band.
scale10k chapters won't lag — the windowing already exists. usePlanWindows keyset-pages each expanded arc's chapters at CHILD_PAGE=100 (the 100 / 340 chip + + 240 more); a collapsed arc loads NOTHING (just a rollup card from the shell). So cost is bounded by what you've expanded, not the book size. Two rules this redesign MUST keep: (1) never default-expand every arc on a big book — that would fetch 100×N at once; expansion stays opt-in / bounded. (2) scenes load only for an expanded chapter. The wrap + resize are purely visual; they ride ON the existing keyset windows, they don't replace them.
