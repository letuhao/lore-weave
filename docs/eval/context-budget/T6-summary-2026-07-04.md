# T6 D6 — fact-preserving extractive summary (live proof, 2026-07-04)

**Change:** the compaction summarizer (`compact_service._SUMMARY_SYSTEM_PROMPT`) now emits
a two-section **FACTS / SYNOPSIS** structure — an explicit verbatim fact list (entities,
decisions, established state, open threads) is the system of record; the prose is the
convenience. Names must stay EXACT (no paraphrase/translate). max_tokens 700→900 for headroom.

**Why:** a lossy prose rolling summary silently drops load-bearing facts a later turn still
needs — worst on the weak local models we target. The FACTS block is what makes lossy
compaction safe (anything listed survives).

## Live proof (gemma-4-26b, in-container `summarize_for_compaction`)

Input transcript planted 4 load-bearing facts (a VN name, a cursed-blade name, a ch3 betrayal
decision, an open "is it sentient?" thread). Output:

```
FACTS:
- Entities: Lâm Uyển, Crimson Codex, Elder Thanh, Black Spire.
- Decisions: Chapter 3 climax involves Lâm Uyển betraying Elder Thanh at the Black Spire.
- Established: Lâm Uyển is the protagonist; Crimson Codex is a cursed blade.
- Open threads: Is the Crimson Codex sentient?
SYNOPSIS: The protagonist, Lâm Uyển, wields a cursed blade known as the Crimson Codex… betrays
her master, Elder Thanh, at the Black Spire… sentience… remains unresolved.
```

**Fact retention: 4/4 KEPT** — `Lâm Uyển` (VN name verbatim, not translated), `Crimson Codex`,
`Elder Thanh`, `Black Spire`. Structure present (FACTS + SYNOPSIS). ✅ PASS.
