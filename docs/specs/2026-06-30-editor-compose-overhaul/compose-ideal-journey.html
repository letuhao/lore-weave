Compose — the ideal story journey

How a person who can't write goes from a blank page to a finished chapter — guided, never lost. This is the experience we design toward; we then POC the current source against it to find the missing pieces.

😶‍🌫️
"I tried Gemma-4 26B to help me brainstorm, but it didn't really help. I still feel empty and don't know how to start."
— the user (an engineer, not a novelist)
Why a raw chat fails: brainstorming with no destination just makes floating ideas. The ideal journey gives every idea somewhere to go — straight into structure, then prose.
Always present — the two things that hold the journey together

The AI Assistant (the core)

A persistent chat — your co-writer and guide. Not a blank box: it runs ideation skills, can call tools, and at every phase knows what you're doing and what's next. It's the front door (Idea) and your companion through Draft.

The Journey rail (where am I / what next)

A small always-visible progress path showing the 6 phases, your current step, and a single "do this next" action. The 24 tools live in the toolbox grouped by these same phases — so the map and the guidance are one thing.

The journey — six phases, each a toolbox group
📄

START · The blank page

FEELING: empty, intimidated, "where do I even begin?"

Instead of 24 tabs, Compose opens on a command center: one warm greeting from the assistant and one button — "Let's find your story." No jargon, no panels yet.

FIX vs today: today you land in an empty studio with 24 tabs and three empty dropdowns. The ideal opens on a single guided entry.
↓
💡

① IDEA · Find the spark

FEELING: curious — "oh, it's asking ME the questions."
You do
Answer a few guided prompts: a genre, a "what if", a character you like.
AI role
Runs a Brainstorm-a-premise skill: proposes loglines, sharpens the hook, names a protagonist.
Tool
AI chat (CoWriter)
Output
A saved premise / logline + a few characters.
FIX vs raw Gemma: this isn't a blank chat. It's a structured, skill-driven interview whose output is a concrete premise the next phase consumes.
NEXT → "Turn this into a structure."
↓
🏗️

② STRUCTURE · Give it a skeleton

FEELING: oriented — "I can see the shape now."
You do
Pick a structure template (3-Act, Hero's Journey…) explained in plain words; review the proposed outline.
AI role
Decomposes premise + template into chapters → scenes, with beats, tension, and cast.
Tool
Planner (+ Beats, Scene Graph)
Output
An outline of scenes you can edit/reorder.
FIX (engine gotcha): the Planner can't mint chapters — so the journey auto-creates the first chapter for a fresh book before decomposing. The user never sees the seam.
NEXT → "Write your opening scene" (Bible is optional and can wait).
↓
🌍

③ STORY BIBLE · Your cast & world (optional · grows itself)

FEELING: reassured — "it remembers my world so I don't have to."
You do
Optionally jot a canon rule ("magic has a cost"). Otherwise: nothing.
AI role
Fills the bible from your prose (extraction): cast, places, relationships, timeline.
Tools
Cast · World · Timeline · Arc · Canon · References
Output
Grounding the draft stays consistent with — auto-maintained.
Non-blocking — you can draft now and let the bible build as you go.
↓
✍️

④ DRAFT · Write, scene by scene

FEELING: flowing — "I'm actually writing a book."
You do
Pick a scene, optionally steer with a guide, hit Generate; Accept / edit / regenerate.
AI role
Drafts the scene grounded in premise + outline + bible; the chat is right there to discuss & re-steer.
Tool
Compose (+ AI chat)
Output
Scene prose you own — accept or rewrite.
NEXT → repeat per scene; the rail tracks "3 of 8 scenes drafted."
↓
🔍

⑤ REFINE · Make it good

FEELING: in control — "it catches what I'd miss."
You do
Review flagged issues; tune voice/density; dismiss or fix.
AI role
Judges coherence, voice, pacing, canon; suggests next moves.
Tools
Critic · Style/Voice · Quality · Motifs
Output
A scene that holds together with the rest of the book.
NEXT → mark scenes done.
↓
📕

⑥ ASSEMBLE · Become a chapter

FEELING: accomplished — "I finished a chapter."
You do
Assemble/stitch the done scenes; publish past the chapter gate.
AI role
Stitches scenes seamlessly; final canon check.
Tool
Assemble / Stitch
Output
A published chapter — and momentum.
NEXT → "Start the next chapter" — now with a premise, a world, and a rhythm.
