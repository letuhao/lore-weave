# Red Team — Work Assistant Mode (2026-07-11)

Four adversarial reviewers (+1 verifier) attacked the three new specs. **Every finding below is
code-verified with file:line.** Verdicts: the KG-indexing spec — *"do not build as written"*; the coaching
spec — *"not ready"*; the data architecture — *"tenancy sound; erasure and temporal models broken at the
foundation."*

**This document is the durable register.** Fixes are applied in the specs; the disposition column says where.

---

## The two cross-cutting insights (worth more than any individual finding)

### ⭐ CC-1 — "Diary taint" must propagate to *derived* stores → **new D16**

Three P0s are **the same shape**: a derived store (the KG, the glossary, the public-MCP surface, notification
bodies, the library list) inherits a permission or lifecycle that was only ever enforced at the **authored**
store (`books`). D10 guarded `books`; **everything downstream of `books` is a separate egress with its own
rules.**

> **One locked rule closes X1, X17, X22 and most of X20:** a row *derived from* a `kind='diary'` source is
> **diary-tainted**, and diary-tainted rows are excluded from **every** list / search / export / notification /
> public-MCP surface unless the caller is the owner in an assistant context.

### ⭐ CC-2 — Supersede · correct · forget-person · rename are **one missing primitive** → **new D17**

X2, X4, X14 and X21 all need the same **three-legged write**:
**(1) amend the PG SSOT → (2) re-index → (3) reconcile the derived graph.**

The spec has *none* of the three legs and treats each as a separate gap. Leg 3 mostly exists
(`maintain_chain`, the `chapter.deleted` cascade). **Leg 1 is missing, and nobody noticed because
`memory_forget` *looks* like it's enough — it isn't** (it touches one Neo4j node, never PG; a rebuild
resurrects the "corrected" fact). Build **memory amendment** once; four P0/P1 cases collapse into one
well-tested path.

## P1 — real holes (selected; full list in the agent transcripts)

| # | Finding | Disposition |
|---|---|---|
| T18 | **The user changes jobs** → the KG blends ex-employer confidential info into the new job ("last time the margin was 34%"). No epoch/org boundary exists. Trade-secret contamination with the assistant as vector. | **New: employment epoch** on the work project; recall defaults to current epoch; export-then-purge at the boundary |
| T19 | **The operator is not the adversary the design defends against.** "Private" means private from other *users*, not from the *operator* — and the likeliest deployment of a **work** assistant is one the **employer** controls. | Honest disclosure at provisioning; scope per-user envelope encryption **now** (it constrains the KG/embedding design) even if built later |
| T20 | **Sweep-all (D8) vs the ≤10 decision budget (E7)** — a 3-week holiday = 21 distiller jobs (60–300 LLM calls, blowing the daily lane) + 21 draft entries to review. | T21 | **Changing timezone/day-cutoff retroactively re-buckets history** → the sweep predicate is not stable under a setting change → duplicate/overlapping entries + partial-unique collisions. | **Stamp `chat_messages.local_date` at write time**; sweep becomes an indexed anti-join; past days keep their assignment |

## P2 (recorded, not lost)

Backups resurrect erased days (14-day retention vs the erasure promise) · DST day-cutoff arithmetic (23h/25h
days) · a vacation registers as a "journaling gap" the coach nags about · the **user's own name becomes a
colleague entity** · export/leave is asserted but undesigned (and is the trust anchor that makes people
willing to start) · `compact_summary` is a *second* undeleted digest of the day · glossary `evidences.original_text`
holds verbatim chapter quotes with no FK · **MinIO objects are never purged** on chapter delete.

## What the red team could NOT break (verified sound)

- **Tenancy on every new store** — no unscoped row, no System-tier mutable row; the diary's partial unique
  correctly exempts tombstones; `knowledge_pending_facts` is `user_id`-filtered on every path.
- **Additive contract fields + the 45s grant cache** — safe: all six projection consumers ignore unknown
  keys, and since `kind` is server-set and immutable, a stale cached `kind` is impossible.
- **The `chapter.kg_indexed` event plumbing** — no new stream or consumer group needed; the relay is
  payload-opaque with no allowlist; unregistered types are cleanly skipped.
- **The backfill causes no re-parse storm** (proven, not asserted) — but see T28 for the one case where
  "behaviorally identical" is false.
- **`temperature=0.0`** is already correct in the evaluator.
